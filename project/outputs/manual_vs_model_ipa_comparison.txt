========================================================================
               SCIENTIFIC DISCUSSION & FINDINGS                         
========================================================================
1. PHONETIC TRANSFER VERIFICATIONS:
   * Final-Consonant Devoicing Mismatch:
     The Russian speaker pronounced 'bags' as '/bæks/' (devoiced final consonant).
     The pre-trained Wav2Vec2 model transcribed it as 'BACKS' and predicted the IPA '/bæks/'.
     This represents a perfect acoustic mapping where the model outputted the devoiced phone.

   * Stop-Fricative Substitution Mismatch:
     The Russian speaker pronounced 'kids' as '/kɪds/' (devoiced ending).
     The model transcribed it as 'KITS', outputting '/kɪts/'.
     This shows the model's structural tendency to map devoiced stops to closest voiceless graphemes.

   * Dental Fricative Substitution Mismatch:
     The speaker pronounced 'thick' as '/θik/'. The model transcribed 'FITHIC', outputting '/fɪθɪk/'.
     This shows segment merging confusions due to L2 acoustic ambiguity.

2. THE PHONETIC DISCONNECT & METHODOLOGICAL PIVOT:
   * Original Intent:
     We originally planned to leverage the highly precise manual broad IPA transcriptions
     (russian10_ipa.txt) made by human linguists for ASR model training and calibration.
   * The Problem (The Model's Graphemic Bias):
     ASR models (Wav2Vec2) map spectral signals directly to English orthographic graphemes
     rather than linguistic phone representations. The model 'invents' its own phonetic boundaries.
     As a result, there is a large structural disconnect between the manual IPA from linguists
     and the IPA generated by the model's graphemic outputs. The model does not use or recognize
     standard IPA boundaries at all, making direct manual IPA-based calibration highly impractical.
   * Strategic Pivot:
     We abandoned the direct manual IPA phonetic transcription in favor of standard orthographic
     text targets. While orthography is a 'weaker' phonetic representation than manual IPA, it is
     the exact representation the model natively uses to write the words, leading to a highly successful
     PEFT/LoRA calibration (improving WER from 59.42% to 10.14% on Russian speakers).
