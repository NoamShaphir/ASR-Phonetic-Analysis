--- CRITICAL RESEARCH UPDATES & ALGORITHMIC CORRECTIVES ---

A. IMPLICIT PRIOR & PHONOTACTIC EXPECTATIONS IN THE ACOUSTIC MODEL (AM):
   * The Acoustic Model (e.g. Wav2Vec2 transformer) is not a pure acoustic representation.
   * Through exposure to thousands of hours of speech during training, the AM learns
     an implicit language prior and phonotactic rules (vowel-consonant expectations).
   * In THINGS (/θɪŋz/), L2 Russian devoicing of /z/ to /s/ produces /θɪŋs/.
   * Because /ŋs/ is phonotactically disallowed/extremely rare in English, the AM itself
     'hallucinates' the voiceless stop /k/, shifting the acoustic vector to /ŋks/ (THINKS).
   * This is a hybrid error where the AM's implicit prior and the search space conspire
     to output a valid lexical word, rather than an external LM correction alone.

B. CONTEXT-SENSITIVE BAYESIAN NOISY CHANNEL DECODING:
   * Modelling L2 transfers with a flat, global static transition probability scalar
     (e.g., P_Trans(/t/ | /θ/) = 0.180) is an oversimplification that introduces a new static heuristic.
   * Physical interdental stopping is highly context-dependent, modeled as P_Trans(sp | int, C).
   * Context variable C represents syntactic position, vowel environments, and surrounding phones.
   * For example, stopping /θ/ -> /t/ is highly active (up to 80%) in word-initial positions
     before front vowels, but drops below 5% in word-medial or consonant cluster environments.
   * The search engine queries a dynamic, context-conditioned transition matrix P_Trans(sp | int, C)
     at each prefix expansion state, resolving complex coarticulatory variations.

C. ASR ALGORITHMIC ALIGNMENT SHIPS AS DIRECT SYMPTOMS OF L2 PROSODY:
   * The 32.4% ASR Algorithmic Alignment Shifts (blank/skipped boundaries) do not occur in a vacuum.
   * The CTC decoder fails at segment alignments and inserts blank tokens because of L2 acoustic features.
   * L2 speech is characterized by hesitations, micro-second silent pauses, and prosodic/temporal lengthening
     as speakers struggle to plan and execute non-native articulatory postures.
   * Thus, these alignment 'artifacts' are direct physiological symptoms of L2 acoustic and timing anomalies.
